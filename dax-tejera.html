How Did Dax Tejera Die?

Dax Tejera (born January 24, 1985) was a 37-year-old executive producer at ABC News whose death shocked everyone. The news of his demise spread like wildfire, and everyone was so shocked that they could not believe that such a wonderful person like him had left the world at such a young age.

Dax’s untimely death raised many questions in everyone’s mind. As the news broke, it came to light that Tejera died of a heart attack. According to ABC news Dax died on Friday, December 22.

Mr. Dax worked in the media industry for 14 years. According to his LinkedIn profile, he started his journey working as a news associate at NBCUniversal Media, LLC. In 2017, he joined ABC News as a Senior Producer and had been in the position of Executive Producer for only 2 years.

How Old was Dax Tejera?

On January 24, 1985, Dax Tejera (age 37) took birth in Washington, District of Columbia, United States. According to sources, he was the first child of his parents before his brother Justin.

Growing up in Miami, Dax spent a wonderful childhood and did schooling at Palmer Trinity School. Following school, he joined Dartmouth College and graduated with a bachelor’s degree in history.

Many thought that he would become a lawyer just like his dad, but his young blood motivated him to do something different and challenging. At first, he was confused about his career path, but after joining NBC Universal as News Associate, his vision became clearer.

Dax Tejera’s Career

For 5 years until 2013, Dax worked at NBC as an associate but in different positions. For the first 9 months, he did the job of an associate; for the next 11 months, he was a researcher, and by the time he left NBC, it had been 3 years since he was working as a producer. In January 2013, Tejera got headhunted by Fusion Media Network, where he thrived.

Meanwhile, he completed the Punch Sulzberger Program at Columbia University – Graduate School of Journalism. In February 2017, Dax left Fusion Media Network and joined the ABC family. He joined as a Senior Producer and worked in Washington D.C. Metro Area. In 2020 he got a promotion and got selected as the executive producer for “This Week with George Stephanopoulos.” Life is going fantastic for him. He had whatever an ordinary man could dream of.
