Biography of Alexei Navalny

Alexei Navalny, a Russian opposition leader and lawyer, has devoted his career to fighting corruption in Russia, facing numerous challenges and threats from the government.

Born on June 4, 1976, in Butyn, Russian Soviet Republic, Navalny graduated with a law degree from the Peoples’ Friendship University of Russia in 1998. He continued his studies at the Financial University, graduating in 2001.

Navalny initiated his legal career in 1998, working for various Russian companies. In 2000, he joined the Russian United Democratic Party Yabloko, entering the political arena. Over the years, he engaged in activism, co-founding movements like DA! – Democratic Alternative and the National Russian Liberation Movement.

His prominence grew in 2011 during mass protests against electoral fraud, resulting in his arrest. Despite challenges, Navalny persisted, leading demonstrations and founding the People’s Alliance in 2013. His Moscow mayoral campaign showcased his resilience, even after facing a five-year suspended sentence.

In 2016, Navalny announced his candidacy for the Russian presidency, sparking anti-corruption demonstrations. Despite arrests and disqualifications, he persisted in organizing protests. His independent investigations into government corruption, published through the Anti-Corruption Foundation, earned him international recognition.

Navalny faced a significant setback in 2020 when he was poisoned with a nerve agent. After recovering in Germany, he returned to Russia in 2021, leading to his imprisonment for parole violations. In 2022, he received an additional 10 to 15 years in prison on new charges.

Throughout his career, Navalny has earned numerous awards for his activism, including the Sakharov Prize and the Knight of Freedom Award. Despite personal sacrifices, he continues to fight for justice and democracy in Russia, considered a prisoner of conscience by Amnesty International.
