Mario Van Peebles Biography

Mario Van Peebles is an American film director and actor best known for directing and starring in New Jack City in the year 1991 and USS Indianapolis: Men of Courage in the year 2016. He was born on January 15, 1957, in Mexico as Mario Cain Van Peebles. Mario is the son of actor and filmmaker Melvin Van Peebles (1932-2021), whom he portrayed in the year 2003 biopic Baadasssss!, which he also co-wrote and directed.

Mario Van Peebles Father

Mario’s father Melvin Peebles (born  August 21, 1932 – September 21, 2021), was an American actor, filmmaker, playwright, novelist, and composer. Melvin worked as an active filmmaker into the 2000s. His feature film debut, The Story of a Three-Day Pass (1967), was based on his own French-language novel La Permission as well was shot in France, as it was difficult for a black American director to get work at the time.

Born Melvin Van Peebles, in Chicago, Illinois, Melvin was the son of Edwin Griffin and Marion Peebles. His father was a tailor. In 1953, he graduated with a B.A. in literature from Ohio Wesleyan University and, thirteen days later, joined the Air Force, serving for three and a half years. Melvin added “Van” to his name when he lived in the Netherlands in his late 20s.

Mario Van Peebles Sister

Megan Van Peebles was a sister of Van Peebles and Max Van Peebles. She was born on June 1, 1958, in San Francisco, California, United States. Megan was an actress, known for Sweet Sweetback’s Baadasssss Song (1971), South Bronx Heroes (1985) as well as Baadasssss! (2003). Megan was married to Ron Van, had two sons: Ronald, Jr. and Roman Van. Megan died on March 13, 2006, in San Mateo, California.
