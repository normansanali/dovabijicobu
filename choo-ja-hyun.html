Early Life

Chu Eun-Ju, also known as Choo Ja Hyun (age 43), is a South Korean actress. She was born on January 20, 1979. Eun-Ju was born and raised in North Gyeongsang Province’s then-capital city of Daegu.

According to sources, Hyun had a troublesome childhood. She was a teenager when her parents divorced. Her dad had gotten remarried, but her mother stayed single. During a Chinese television program, Ja revealed that she had a younger sister who perished by drowning. Sources also say that Hyun’s mother was emotionally traumatized, and she continued to taunt Choo that she should have died instead of her younger sister.

The actress tried to keep her mind out of the hardships and did not let her family conditions affect her studies. She completed middle school at a local institution named Gaebong, after which she graduated from Guil High School. After finishing school studies, Ja earned a bachelor’s degree in theatre and film from Dankook University in 1997.

In 2006, She gained huge fame for her depiction of a damaged drug addict in Bloody Tie, a film noir about the meth trade in 1990s Busan, after spending a decade in relative anonymity. In addition to nominations from the Blue Dragon Film Awards and the Baeksang Arts Awards, she received acting awards from the Grand Bell Awards, the Korean Film Awards, and the Director’s Cut Awards.
