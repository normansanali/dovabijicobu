What team was Jalen Brunson drafted to?

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, September 25, 2024

Jalen Brunson, a standout guard from Villanova, began his NBA career when he was selected by the Dallas Mavericks with the No. 33 pick in the 2018 NBA Draft.

Since then, Brunson has carved out an impactful role with the Mavericks, showcasing his skills and contributing significantly to the team's success.

Let's delve deeper into Jalen Brunson's NBA draft journey with the Dallas Mavericks, analyzing his career progression, playing style, and contributions to the team.

Draft Selection and Initial Assessment

After an impressive college career at Villanova, where Brunson showcased his leadership and playmaking abilities, the Mavericks saw potential in him and decided to draft him in 2018.

At the time of his selection, there were concerns about Brunson's physical attributes, particularly his size, wingspan, and athleticism.

Some analysts pointed out that he might struggle with speedier opponents and could be limited to defending the point guard position in the NBA. However, the Mavericks viewed Brunson's skills, basketball IQ, and intangibles as valuable assets for their team.

Player Comparison and Offensive Strengths

In terms of playing style, Brunson has drawn comparisons to established NBA players such as Jameer Nelson and Andre Miller.

His offensive prowess was evident early on, as he displayed remarkable efficiency and productivity on the court.

Brunson excelled in various offensive aspects, including pick-and-roll ball-handling, three-point shooting, isolation scoring, and post play.

His high basketball IQ was reflected in his decision-making and passing, where he consistently made the right plays and demonstrated an ability to create scoring opportunities for his teammates.

Despite some limitations in his transition game and explosiveness, Brunson's offensive skills and scoring efficiency made him a promising prospect for the Mavericks.

Defensive Outlook and Rookie-Year Projection

While Brunson's offensive capabilities were well-regarded, questions arose regarding his defensive abilities.

His heavy-footedness and defensive statistics in isolation and chasing opponents off screens raised concerns about his capacity to contain faster NBA guards. Additionally, his defensive playmaking ability was deemed minimal, with relatively low steal and block numbers.

Despite these defensive challenges, the Mavericks anticipated that Brunson could earn backup minutes early in his rookie year, leveraging his shooting and decision-making skills.

It was projected that he could become a high-end backup and contribute as a second-unit game manager, given his experience and ability to run an offense effectively.

Career Progression and Impact with the Mavericks

Following his draft selection, Brunson spent three seasons as a backup before transitioning to a starting role in the 2021-22 season. His pivotal contributions played a significant part in the Mavericks' journey to the Western Conference Finals in 2022.

His performance and impact on the team ultimately led to a notable $104 million contract with the New York Knicks. Despite initial doubts surrounding the contract, Brunson's on-court excellence and emergence as a star player solidified his value and contribution to the team.

Redrafting and Recognition

In a redraft of the 2018 NBA Draft in 2023, Brunson's potential and impact were further acknowledged as he was selected sixth overall, trailing only behind prominent names such as Luka Doncic, Shai Gilgeous-Alexander, Trae Young, Jaren Jackson, and Mikal Bridges.

The recognition of Brunson's abilities and impact in the league highlighted his progression from a second-round pick to a player with a case for a top-three selection.

 

All in all, Jalen Brunson's NBA draft journey with the Dallas Mavericks reflects a story of perseverance, skill development, and impactful contributions to the team.

Despite initial concerns about his physical attributes and defensive capabilities, Brunson's offensive prowess, basketball IQ, and leadership qualities have propelled him to a prominent position in the league.

His progression from a backup player to a key contributor in the Mavericks' success and subsequent recognition as a top talent in the league underscore his resilience and impact.
