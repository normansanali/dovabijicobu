Inside The Life Of famous American Commentator Van Jones

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Saturday, September 14, 2024

Inside The Life Of famous American Commentator Van Jones- Van Jones, an American aged 54 is an author, lawyer, news and political commentator, and co-founder of several non-profit organizations. He is most known for being a three-time New York Times bestselling author, a CNN host and contributor, and an Emmy Award winner.

Jones served as President Barack Obama’s Special Advisor for Green Jobs in 2009 and was a distinguished visiting fellow at Princeton University.

Who is Van Jones?

Being the son of Loretta Jean, a high school teacher, and Willie Anthony Jones, a middle school principal, Anthony Kapel Jones who is professionally known as Van Jones was born on September 20, 1968, in Jackson, Tennessee, alongside his twin sister Angela.

Regarding his education, Van had his high school education at Jackson Central-Merry High School in his hometown.

From there he proceeded to have his college education at the University of Tennessee at Martin (UT Martin) where he earned a Bachelor of Science in communication and political science. He also went to Yale Law School and studied law.

What is Van Jones’s age, height, and weight?

Van who was born on September 20, 1968, is 54 years old. He stands at a height of 5 feet 7 inches and weighs around 70kg.

What is Van Jones’s net worth?

As a successful political commentator, activist, entrepreneur, lawyer, and author, Jones has earned an estimated net worth of $5 million.

What is Van Jones’s Nationality and Ethnicity?

Jones is an American national and belongs to an Afro-American ethnic background.

What is Van Jones’s profession?

Van Jones established the Ella Baker Center for Human Rights, an umbrella NGO, in 1996. Jones led a campaign against Proposition 21 in 1999 and 2000, which aimed to increase penalties for violent crimes and require more juvenile offenders to be tried as adults. Despite Jones’ efforts to mobilize a student protest movement, the proposition was passed, contributing to the high rates of incarceration, particularly among minorities, in the US.

In response, the Ella Baker Center launched the “Books Not Bars” campaign in 2001, which aimed to block the construction of a proposed “Super-Jail for Youth” in Oakland’s Alameda County. The campaign later expanded statewide to reform California’s juvenile justice system.During the 2003 California gubernatorial recall election, Jones served as Arianna Huffington’s statewide grassroots director.Jones, a former Obama administration official, has been a regular contributor to CNN since the end of his political debate show, Crossfire. He has contributed to segments on a wide range of topics, including Obama administration policies, Supreme Court decisions, protests in Ferguson, Missouri, and the 2016 Republican presidential primary.

In 2018, he launched The Van Jones Show on CNN, with Jay-Z as his first guest. Jones also launched The Redemption Project with Van Jones, a show focused on restorative justice. He has defended Tulsi Gabbard against Hillary Clinton’s accusations and commented on the racist behavior of white liberals. Jones is the President of The Dream Corps, a social enterprise and incubator for powerful ideas and innovations designed to uplift and empower the most vulnerable in our society.

Why does Van Jones wear purple?

According to him, he stated, ” I also try to wear a purple tie as often as I can, as a tribute to my friend Prince, who, in addition to being a rock star, did a lot of good work creating opportunities for people.”

Who is Van Jones married to?

Van was once upon a time in a union with Jana Carter, they tied the knot in 2005. Unfortunately, they divorced in 2019 and moved on with their lives. However, together they have produced two children, Mattai Jones and Cabral Jones.
